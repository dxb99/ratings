How Scoring Works

Scores are calculated only from submitted ratings. Missing votes are ignored and are not counted as zero.

Average Average means the normal score after everyone who voted is added together. First, add the scores. Then divide by how many people voted. Example: if a player gets 8, 7, 9, 6, and 8, the total is 38. Since 5 people voted, 38 / 5 = 7.6. So the average is 7.6.
Median Median means the middle score. Put the scores in order from lowest to highest, then look for the score in the middle. Example: 5, 6, 7, 8, 10 has 7 in the middle, so the median is 7. If there are two middle scores, use the number halfway between them. Example: 6, 7, 8, 9 has 7 and 8 in the middle, so the median is 7.5.
Weighted Score Weighted score is only for Version 3. It means some categories count more than others. Combat Skills counts 22%, Movement / Speed counts 18%, Map Awareness counts 18%, Decision Making counts 17%, Team Impact counts 15%, and Communication counts 10%. Example: a 10 in Combat helps more than a 10 in Communication because Combat has more weight. If all six category scores are 8, the weighted score is also 8.

How Version 3 Works

Each player receives six category scores. The system averages those six numbers to produce one final player rating.

Example: 10, 7.5, 7.5, 5, 10, 7.5 gives a final rating of 7.9.
